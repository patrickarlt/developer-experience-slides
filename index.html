(use ↔ to navigate) # What Makes a Great Developer Service?
Great developer services help developers build better apps. They do this by solving problems for developers. Do something developers don't want to do on their own. Own your problem space.
Be the best at solving **YOUR** problem. Developers will use your service because it lets then focus on
devivering the best products for **THEIR** users and customers # Great Developer Services ### Hosting ![Heroku](img/heroku.jpg) ![Amazon Web Services](img/aws.jpg) ### Cost Managment ![Cloudability](img/Cloudability.jpg) ### Mobile Backends ![Parse](img/Parse.jpg) ![Stackmob](img/StackMob.jpg) ### Email ![Mandrill](img/mandrill.jpg) [SendGrid](img/sendgrid.jpg) ### Communications [Twilio](img/twilio.jpg) # What problems can Esri solve for developers? ## Mapping

Developers should be able to use your
product to do **SOMETHING** within 5 minutes
  • Provide sample code
  • Getting started tutorials
  • Full and complete documentation
[Parse Getting Started](img/parse-guides.png) [Parse Sample Code](img/parse-samples.png) [Stripe Docs](img/stripe.png) ## Dont Waste Developers Time * Short signup process * Get back writing code ASAP [Github Signup](img/github-signup.png) [Github Onboarding](img/github-onbarding.png) ## Show Dont Tell
Don't **TELL** me your service can do something
**SHOW** me your service can do something [Twilio Demos](img/twilio-demos.png) Don't **TELL** it's easy
**SHOW** me it's easy [Heroku Home Page](heroku-sample-code.png) ## Progressive Disclosure
Developers don't care if your service can do A,B,C,D,X,Y,Z. They just want to use it for C Solve their problem first then show them more features they can use... [Heroku How It Works Page](img/heroku-hosting.png) Solve the problem of hosting first... Then show what else you can do... [Heroku Addons](img/heroku-addons.png) ## Documentation IS Marketing
Documentation is a metric of how good a service is ### Is your documentation... * Well written? * Well organized? * Written in plain english? - no marketing or special terms ## Don't Sell
Your product is not simple, easy to use, or the best Talk about your product **DOES** Developers will tell other develoeprs how good it is ## APIs are the new UIs
Devlopers want APIs to build with [LEGO](img/lego.jpg) Not user interfaces to interact with Design is how it works not what it looks like ### Lightbulb Test How many lines of code does it take to ________________? make a map on a web page? get directions form A to B? If you don't enjoy using your own system you're doing to wrong Start designing APIs the same way you
would a wireframe or a user interface Think about what developers need to accomplish And the simplest way for them to accomplish it # How developers find your product [Google](img/google.png) 1. Google for how to do something 2. Your product or a post about it comes up [Stack Overflow](img/stackoverflow.png) 1. Ask a question about how to do something 2. Answer is your product [Blogs and News](img/hacker-new.png) 1. Someone posts about how they did X,Y,Z 2. They used your product 3. Someone else needs to do X,Y,Z 3. They use your product [Your Docs](img/search.png) 1. Someone Searches for "geocoding api" 2. Your documentation comes up # In All These cases
Developers want something to solve their **specific** problem
